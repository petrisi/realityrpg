📖 Sample Campaigns

Reality RPG campaigns are structured narratives that span weeks, months, or years of a character's life. Each campaign below provides a complete session-by-session arc with NPCs, timelines, key dice checks, and GM guidance. Use these as-is or adapt them to your table's needs.

💡 How to Run a Campaign

Each session represents a time block chosen by the GM — typically 1–4 weeks of in-game time. Between sessions, advance the calendar, resolve background events, and let characters' SP (Stress Points) recover during downtime. Remember: 2d10 + modifier vs TN is the core resolution for every action. Max SP = 20 + RES mod × 5.

🎓 Campaign 1: "The First Year"

📋 Campaign Overview

Premise: A 22-year-old recent graduate leaves home for the first time. Over 12 sessions (roughly one year of in-game time), they navigate independence — finding housing, landing a first job, building a social circle, experiencing love and loss, and ultimately making a defining life choice.

Recommended PC: New college graduate, age 22. Starting VIG 8, INT 9, EXP 5, DXT 8, RES 7, PRC 7 (adjust for your character). SP max: 20 + (−3) × 5 = 5 — very fragile at first, but growth is expected.

Tone: Coming-of-age. Hopeful but honest about the difficulty of early adulthood.

⚖️ GM Notes: "The First Year"

Pacing: Sessions 1–3 are fast and overwhelming. Sessions 4–6 slow down as relationships deepen. Sessions 7–10 raise the stakes. Sessions 11–12 provide resolution. Growth: Award +1 to an attribute at Session 11 if the player demonstrates meaningful character development. RES is the most likely candidate.

Resolution

• 2d10 + RES vs TN 12 — Articulate what you've learned
• 2d10 + PRC vs TN 11 — Share your story with someone who needs it
• 2d10 + INT vs TN 10 — Set intentions for the future
• 2d10 + VIG vs TN 9 — Feel physically strong for the first time in months
SP: −5 (catharsis). Campaign ends on a note of earned peace.
💡 GM Notes: "Midlife Reset"

This campaign is emotionally heavy. Establish strong safety tools before play. Use the lines and veils approach — some tables may want to fade-to-black on the divorce details or health specifics. The arc should feel earned, not easy. Avoid melodrama; the power is in quiet moments of courage.

🌍 Campaign 3: "The Immigrant Dream"

📋 Campaign Overview

Premise: A family — parents and one or two children — has recently moved to a new country. Over 12 sessions, they face language barriers, cultural dislocation, legal hurdles, and the slow, hard work of building a new life. The theme is belonging — what it costs, what it means, and what it feels like to finally arrive.

Recommended PCs: Play each family member as a PC (2–3 players) OR play the primary breadwinner with family as complex NPCs. Starting stats vary by role. Parent: VIG 8, INT 8, EXP 10, DXT 7, RES 9, PRC 7. SP max: 20 + (+4) × 5 = 40.

Tone: Bittersweet, hopeful, culturally rich. Honor the real experiences of immigrant families.

⚠️ GM Notes: "The Immigrant Dream"

The immigrant experience is not monolithic. Some tables may want to include specific topics (deportation, discrimination, religious persecution) — use content warnings and safety tools before every session that touches on them.

⚖️ GM Notes: "The Activist"

The ending is not predetermined. Let the dice and player choices determine victory or defeat. Both are narratively satisfying. If the PC wins: show the cost — relationships strained, health impacted, personal life neglected. If the PC loses: show the seeds — the community is organized, awareness is raised, the next fight is easier. The GM should never rail-road the outcome. Let the players earn whatever happens.

🎯 Running Reality RPG Campaigns: GM Tips

📐 Time & Pacing

Each session should advance 1–4 weeks of in-game time. Between sessions, resolve background events: bills arrive, relationships evolve, health changes, seasons shift. Use the Seasonal Calendar (GM Tools page) to track monthly events that affect all characters.

🎲 Mechanical Growth

Characters should grow over a campaign. At the end of every 3 sessions, allow the player to increase one attribute by +1 if they've demonstrated meaningful development in that area. SP max recalculates with new RES. Track EXP gains through real skill-building (language classes, work experience, therapy).

⚖️ SP Management

Max SP = 20 + RES mod × 5. Characters recover 1 SP per week of downtime and 3 SP per week of genuine rest (vacation, meditation, therapy). SP overload (exceeding max) triggers a stress event — see Mental Health for details. Use SP as a narrative tool, not a punishment.

🎭 NPC Complexity

Every NPC in a Reality RPG campaign should have at least two dimensions. The "bad" boss has a sick child. The "perfect" partner hides an addiction. The "wise" mentor makes a crucial mistake. Real people are contradictory — your NPCs should be too.
