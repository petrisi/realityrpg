Born in Chicago to Nigerian immigrant parents. His father is a taxi driver, his mother works double shifts at a hospital cleaning crew. Jamal was the first in his family to even think about college — he went to community college for two years studying business administration before money ran out and he had to take a full-time barista job to help pay rent. He's now taking night classes one at a time, saving for a transfer to a four-year school. His parents are proud but push him hard — every family gathering includes at least three aunts asking "so when are you going to get your real degree?" He loves his job — the regulars are like family, and he's genuinely good at making people's mornings better. He wakes up at 4:30 AM every day without an alarm.
