🎲 Core Mechanics

The engine that powers every action in Reality RPG. If you understand this page, you understand the game.

1. The Core Resolution: The d100 System

Almost every action in Reality RPG is resolved by rolling 2d10 (one percent die, or two d10s read as 1–100) and adding a modifier. The result is compared to a Target Number (TN).

Roll TypeResult
Critical SuccessRoll ≤ 5 (before modifiers). Extraordinary, almost lucky outcome.
Strong SuccessRoll + modifier ≤ TN − 5. The action succeeds exceptionally well.
SuccessRoll + modifier ≤ TN. The action succeeds normally.
Partial SuccessRoll + modifier ≤ TN + 5. Succeeds but with a cost or complication.
FailureRoll + modifier > TN + 5. The action fails.
Critical FailureRoll ≥ 96 (before modifiers). Catastrophic, almost unlucky outcome.
📝 Example: Applying for a Job

Mei rolls to interview well. Her TN is 55 (a medium-difficulty social task). She rolls 2d10 = 38, adds her Communication modifier (+12), for a total of 50. This is ≤ 55, so it's a Success. She makes a good impression. If she'd rolled a 12 (total 24), that would be a Critical Success — the interviewer is genuinely impressed and fast-tracks her.

2. The Six Core Attributes

Every human being in Reality RPG is defined by six core attributes, rated from 1 to 20. The modifier is calculated as (Attribute ÷ 5), rounded down. These cover every dimension of human capability.

3. Skills

Skills represent learned competencies. Each skill is tied to one or more attributes. A character's Skill Modifier = Attribute Mod + Skill Level. Skill levels range from −1 (untrained penalty) to +5 (expert).

4. Health System

Instead of hit points, characters track Health on a condition track from Healthy to Dead. Damage comes from physical harm, illness, exhaustion, and neglect.

ConditionPenaltyDescription
😊 HealthyNoneFeeling great. No penalties.
😐 Tired−5 on VIG checksPoor sleep, minor illness, overwork. Very common.
😫 Sick/Exhausted−10 on all checksFever, severe fatigue, dehydration, hangover.
🤒 Injured/Ill−15 on all checks, cannot exertBroken bone, hospitalization, severe infection.
🏥 CriticalCannot act without helpLife-threatening. Needs immediate medical care.
💀 Dead—Character has died.

Recovery requires rest, medical care, and time. A character who sleeps 8 hours in a clean bed moves from Tired to Healthy. Moving from Sick to Tired takes 1–3 days of rest. Injuries may require hospital visits and weeks of recovery.

5. Stress and Mental Strain

Characters accumulate Stress Points (SP) from traumatic events, chronic pressure, conflict, and hardship. The maximum Stress capacity equals 20 + RES modifier × 5. Characters start each day at 0 SP unless an ongoing condition has loaded them.

Stress LevelSP RangeEffects
😌 Stable0No penalty. Character functions normally.
😐 Elevated1–5Minor distraction. −2 on RES checks.
😟 Stressed6–15Noticeable impairment. −5 on RES and INT checks. Must make a RES check (TN 40) each night for restful sleep.
😰 Overwhelmed16–25Significant dysfunction. −8 on all checks. Risk of Episode if SP exceeds max.
💥 Episode> Max SPCharacter enters an Episode. Condition-specific effects apply. See Mental Health chapter.

Stress reduces through rest (−1 SP per good night's sleep), social support (−2 SP per meaningful conversation), exercise (−1 SP per workout), therapy (−3 to −5 SP per session), and time away from stressors.

💡 Unified Stress System

The core-mechanics stress system and the mental-health system use the same SP pool. Characters without a diagnosed condition can still accumulate stress from life events. Characters with a condition have additional triggers, episode mechanics, and recovery options.

6. Time System

Time is the most precious resource in Reality RPG. The GM tracks time at the granularity appropriate to the scene:

MinutesTense moments, confrontations, emergenciesA fight, a job interview, a medical emergency

Weeks/MonthsLong-term arcs, career progressionTraining for a marathon, saving for a house

7. Target Numbers

Standard TNs for common activities. The GM adjusts based on circumstances.

DifficultyTNExamples
Trivial25Brushing teeth, making toast, walking to the store
Easy35Cooking a simple meal, sending an email, small talk
Medium50Having a difficult conversation, fixing a leak, studying for a test
Hard65Getting a promotion, convincing a stranger, assembling complex furniture
Very Hard80Winning a major award, landing a dream job, surviving a natural disaster
Near Impossible90Becoming a CEO, winning a national competition, escaping prison
⚠️ Circumstance Modifiers

Every situation has modifiers. Being tired adds +5 to TN. Having the right tools subtracts 5. Emotional distress adds +10. A supportive ally subtracts 5. The GM should always consider context before setting a TN.
