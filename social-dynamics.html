Social Dynamics

Relationships, communication, conflict, romance, family, reputation, and community — the invisible architecture of human life.

📜 Core Principle

In Reality RPG, social connections are as mechanically vital as health or money. A person with no relationships suffers real, quantifiable penalties to mental health, opportunity access, and daily functioning. Conversely, a rich social network provides buffers against stress, doors to opportunity, and meaning itself. Every person in the game — PCs and NPCs alike — navigates the same social systems.

Social dynamics in Reality RPG are governed by three interlocking systems: Relationships (who you know and how close you are), Interactions (how you communicate and resolve conflicts), and Social Capital (your standing in the wider world). These systems feed into each other — a broken relationship costs social capital, and social capital can help repair relationships.

1. Relationship System 🧬

Every person your character has meaningfully encountered is tracked at one of seven relationship tiers. The tier determines what mechanical benefits, obligations, and narrative weight that connection carries.

Relationship Tiers

👤 Stranger — Default state for anyone not yet encountered. No benefits, no obligations.
👋 Acquaintance — Exchanged names; brief conversation. 1d6 at +1. Minor favors only.
🤙 Friend — Shared meaningful experiences; met 3+ times. 1d6 at +2. +1 Mental Health when together.
💛 Close Friend — Vulnerability shared; 6+ months consistent. 1d6 at +3. +2 Mental Health. Will answer at 2 AM.
🤫 Confidant — Deep secrets shared; trust proven. 1d6 at +4. +3 Mental Health. Will lie to protect you.
❤️ Partner/Spouse — Romantic commitment; shared life. 1d6 at +5. +4 Mental Health. Full resource integration.
🏠 Family-Level Bond — Blood or chosen family; unconditional. 1d6 at +5. +4 Mental Health. Cannot be unfriended, only estranged.
Tier How It Forms Mechanical Benefits Obligations
Stranger 👤 Default state for anyone not yet encountered None None
Acquaintance 👋 Exchanged names; brief conversation; seen in passing multiple times Can ask for minor, low-effort favors. 1d6 at +1. Minimal — a polite greeting if you pass each other
Friend 🤙 Shared a meaningful experience; reached out voluntarily; met 3+ times 1d6 at +2. +1 to Mental Health when spending time together. Can call for a ride, borrow items, ask for advice. Expected to respond within 24–48 hours; show up to planned hangouts
Close Friend 💛 Vulnerability shared; supported through difficulty; 6+ months consistent 1d6 at +3. +2 to Mental Health. Will pick up at 2 AM. Can borrow money (up to $100). Will vouch for you. Expected to show genuine support during crises; remember important dates

Deepening Relationships

Relationships deepen through shared time and shared vulnerability. The GM tracks relationship progression using a simple point system:

+1 ☕ Casual conversation — Coffee, passing chat (30 min – 1 hour)
+2 🎮 Shared activity — Movie, games, working out (1–3 hours)
+3 💬 Meaningful conversation — Personal topics, advice, life updates (1–2 hours)
+4 🆘 Shared hardship or crisis — Helping them through something (variable)
+5 🤫 Vulnerability exchange — Sharing a deep secret, admitting fear (30 min – 1 hour)
+6 🎓 Major life event together — Wedding, funeral, graduation, hospital visit (half – full day)

Each tier requires a cumulative threshold: Acquaintance = 3 pts, Friend = 10 pts, Close Friend = 25 pts, Confidant = 50 pts, Partner = 75 pts (plus mutual romantic interest), Family-Level = 100+ pts or blood relation.

⚠️ Relationship Decay

Relationships decay without maintenance. If no contact occurs for more than 30 days, lose 2 points. After 90 days, lose 5 points. After 6 months, the relationship drops one tier. After 1 year of no contact, the person becomes a Stranger again (unless they were Family-Level, which requires explicit estrangement).

📖 Scenario: Building a Friendship
Jamila
Protagonist — Building connections
Communication: +3 Empathy: +2

Jamila notices a quiet colleague, Kevin, eating lunch alone every day. She invites him to join her. They talk about the commute (+1 point). A week later, she asks if he wants to grab dinner after work — they discover they both love the same obscure band (+3 points for meaningful conversation). When Kevin's car breaks down, Jamila drives him home (+4 for shared hardship). After a month of consistent contact, Kevin moves from Stranger → Acquaintance (3 pts) → Friend (10 pts). He now provides +1 to Jamila's Mental Health checks when they hang out, and will help her move apartments next month.

2. Social Interaction Resolution 💬

Not every interaction needs a dice roll — most daily socializing happens automatically. Rolls are called for when there is stakes, difficulty, or ambiguity. The three core social checks are:

Communication Checks 🗣️

Used when you need to persuade, negotiate, explain something complex, or make an important ask. Roll 2d10 + Communication Skill (derived from Presence and Intellect). The GM sets a target number based on difficulty:

5+ 🟢 Easy — Asking a friend to watch your cat while you travel
7+ 🔵 Standard — Pitching a new idea to your boss
9+ 🟠 Hard — Asking your landlord for a rent reduction
10+ 🔴 Very Hard — Convincing a skeptical parent to support your career change
11+ ⚫ Nearly Impossible — Talking a stranger out of calling the police on you

Relationship tier modifiers apply: the closer the person is to you, the lower the target number (−1 per tier above Acquaintance).

Empathy Checks 👁️

Used when you need to read someone's true feelings, detect if someone is lying, understand an unspoken tension, or pick up on social cues. Roll 2d10 + Empathy Skill (derived from Resolve and Presence). Success tells you what the person is feeling, even if they're hiding it.

4− ❌ Fail — You misread the situation entirely. May act inappropriately.
5–6 🔍 Partial — You sense something is "off" but can't pinpoint what
7–8 ✅ Success — You correctly identify the person's dominant emotion
9–10 🧠 Strong — You understand their emotion AND their underlying motivation
11+ 👁️‍🗨️ Exceptional — Full emotional landscape: feelings, wants, and hidden fears

Deception Checks 🎭

Used when you lie, exaggerate, omit the truth, or present a false persona. Roll 2d10 + Deception Skill (derived from Presence and Expression). The target is trying to read you — their Empathy Skill is subtracted from your result.

+3 🟢 You beat them by 3+ — They fully believe you. No suspicion.
+1–2 🟡 You beat them by 1–2 — They believe you, but something feels slightly off
= ⚖️ Tie — They're unsure. May probe further or let it go.
−1–2 🟠 You lose by 1–2 — They suspect you're hiding something
−3+ 🔴 You lose by 3+ — They know you're lying. Consequences follow.
⚠️ Miscommunication Penalty

When a Communication check rolls a 1 (natural), a miscommunication occurs. The GM describes a genuine misunderstanding: you said one thing but they heard another. This can escalate conflicts, create awkwardness, or damage relationships. The GM may require a 1d6 roll to determine severity (1–2 = minor awkwardness, 3–4 = meaningful damage, 5–6 = relationship tier drop).

📖 Scenario: The Awkward Dinner
Carlos
On a first date — nervous but sincere
Presence: +2 Communication: +1

Carlos is on a date with Sophie. He wants to ask if she's interested in a second date, but his Communication check rolls a 1. Instead of "Would you want to do this again?", he blurts out, "So… do you think we should see each other again?" — which sounds like a demand rather than an invitation. Sophie rolls Empathy and succeeds — she understands he's nervous, not pushy, but the moment is still awkward. Carlos loses 1 relationship point for the stumble, but Sophie's empathy saves the interaction from disaster.

3. Conflict Resolution ⚡

Conflict in Reality RPG is rarely resolved with violence. Most conflicts are verbal, social, or institutional. When they escalate to physical violence, the consequences are severe and lasting.

Verbal Conflict — Arguments 🗯️

When two characters are in a heated disagreement, the GM calls for Communication vs. Communication (or Communication vs. Empathy, depending on who's trying to talk and who's trying to understand). The loser of the exchange must either:

  • Concede — back down and let the other person "win" the argument
  • Escalate — raise their voice, make accusations, threaten to leave
  • Disengage — walk away, go silent, refuse to continue

Each round of escalation increases the Stress of both parties by 1 point. Escalation also risks relationship damage — every round of active arguing costs 1 relationship point.

Lv 1 💬 Disagreement — Calm but firm exchange of differing views (Stress: 0, Relationship cost: 0)
Lv 2 🗣️ Heated Discussion — Voices raised, personal points made, still civil (Stress: +1, Cost: 1 pt)
Lv 3 🔥 Argument — Accusations, interruptions, past grievances brought up (Stress: +2, Cost: 2 pts)
Lv 4 💥 Fight (Verbal) — Insults, shouting, threats, character attacks (Stress: +3, Cost: 3–5 pts)
Lv 5 🥊 Physical Violence — Pushing, hitting, throwing things (Stress: +5+, Relationship may drop 1–2 tiers)

Physical Violence — Real Consequences 🥊

⚖️ The Violence Rule

If a character engages in physical violence against another person, the following automatically applies, regardless of who "wins" the physical confrontation:

  • Immediate: Both parties gain 5 Stress. Physical damage resolved per core mechanics.
  • Within 24 hours: The victim may press charges. If they do, the aggressor faces legal consequences — arrest, court appearance, possible conviction, criminal record.
  • Within 1 week: News spreads. Both parties lose Social Capital in their communities. The aggressor loses more.
  • Long-term: The relationship with the victim drops at least 2 tiers (likely to Stranger). Mutual friends may take sides, fracturing social networks.
  • Employment: If the violence was witnessed or reported, the aggressor risks disciplinary action or termination.

Mediation 🕊️

A third party can intervene to de-escalate. The mediator rolls 2d10 + their Communication and Empathy skills combined (divided by 2, rounded up). Success stops the conflict and begins a cooling-off period.

During a cooling-off period (1–7 days, set by GM), the parties do not interact. After this period, they may attempt reconciliation, which requires a Communication check at Hard difficulty (9+) with a bonus from the mediator's relationship tier with each party.

Cooling-Off Periods ❄️

After any conflict reaching Escalation Level 3 or higher, a cooling-off period is mandatory before the relationship can begin healing. During this time:

  • No relationship points can be gained
  • Both parties have −1 to Mental Health checks (lingering stress)
  • If either party attempts to contact the other during cooling-off, the conflict may re-escalate
📖 Scenario: The Roommate Fight
Aisha & Marcus
Roommates for 8 months — Close Friends (28 pts) fighting about unpaid bills
Relationship: 24 pts Stress: +3 each Mediator: DeShawn

Aisha and Marcus, roommates for 8 months (Close Friends, 28 points), have a fight about unpaid bills. It escalates to Level 4 — insults about family, character attacks. Both gain 3 Stress. The relationship loses 4 points (now at 24, teetering on the edge of dropping to Friend). Their mutual friend DeShawn (Confidant to both) intervenes — rolls 2d10 + 7 and gets a 12. Mediation succeeds. A 5-day cooling-off period begins. During those 5 days, Aisha and Marcus text DeShawn but not each other. On day 6, Marcus sends an apology text (Communication check, target 9, rolls 10 — success). Aisha accepts. They don't regain lost points immediately, but the decay stops, and slow rebuilding begins.

4. Romance and Intimacy 💕

Romantic relationships are among the most impactful forces in a character's life. They provide the strongest Mental Health bonuses — and the deepest wounds when they end.

Dating Mechanics 💘

Romantic interest is established through attraction checks and compatibility checks:

Mechanic Roll Purpose
💘 Attraction 2d10 + Presence Determines if the other person finds you romantically interesting. Target 7+. Modifiers for shared values (−1), different life stages (+1 difficulty), cultural compatibility (GM discretion)
🤝 Compatibility 2d10 + Empathy Determines if you can build a lasting connection. Target 7+. Checks shared values, communication styles, life goals alignment
💪 Vulnerability 2d10 + Resolve Each date requires a small vulnerability check — sharing something real. Failure means staying in "small talk" mode, which doesn't build relationship points

Relationship Milestones 🏁

Romantic relationships progress through identifiable milestones. Each milestone is a narrative event that the GM calls out:

☕ First Date — Intentional one-on-one romantic outing. Relationship enters romantic track; points build faster (+2 per date)
🔄 Second Date — They wanted to see you again. Mutual interest confirmed; attraction check no longer needed
💗 "I Like You" — First expression of romantic interest. Relationship points accelerate; vulnerability checks easier (−1 to target)
💍 Exclusive — Agreement to only date each other. Relationship tier increases by 1 (minimum Friend). Fidelity expectations begin
❤️ "I Love You" — First expression of deep romantic commitment. Tier increases by 1 (minimum Close Friend). Mental Health bonus activates
👨‍👩‍👧 Meeting Family — Introduced to each other's family. Relationship deepens; family dynamics now intersect with your network
🏠 Moving In Together — Sharing a living space. Partner tier reached. Finances intertwine. Daily life mechanics now shared
💒 Engagement/Marriage — Formal commitment. Spouse tier. Legal and financial merger. Shared liability. Maximum Mental Health bonus

Breakups and Divorce 💔

When a romantic relationship ends, the mechanical impact is severe:

  • Immediate Stress: 3–8 Stress (set by GM based on relationship length and who initiated)
  • Mental Health penalty: −2 to all Mental Health checks for 4–12 weeks (longer for marriages)
  • Relationship tier: Drops to Acquaintance (if amicable) or Stranger (if hostile)
  • Shared resources: Must be divided. If living together, one person must leave within 2–4 weeks.
  • Mutual friends: May be forced to "pick a side" — 50% chance each mutual friend's relationship with the non-initiating partner drops one tier
⚖️ Consent Framework

At the table: Before any romantic or intimate scene is played, the GM must confirm all players at the table are comfortable. Players may use a X-card (say "X" at any time to stop the scene) or a fade-to-black agreement. Intimate scenes are narrated, not acted out.

In the game world: Consent is always explicit. A character cannot force or coerce another character into any romantic or physical interaction. Attempting to do so is a serious in-game violation with severe consequences — legal, social, and mechanical. The GM will not simulate non-consensual scenarios.

📖 Scenario: The First Date
Priya & James
First date — matched on a dating app
Priya Attraction: 10 ✓ James Attraction: 7 ✓ Priya Vuln: +3 pts James Vuln: +1 pt

Priya and James matched on a dating app. First date at a coffee shop. Priya rolls Attraction: 2d10 (7) + Presence (3) = 10. James rolls Attraction: 2d10 (5) + Presence (2) = 7. Both succeed — mutual attraction confirmed. They then roll Vulnerability: Priya gets a 9 (shares her passion for photography), James gets a 4 (stays in small talk, talks about the weather). Priya gains +3 relationship points (meaningful conversation), but James only gains +1 (superficial). The GM notes that James needs to open up more on the second date for the connection to deepen.

5. Family Dynamics 🏠

Family — whether by blood, marriage, or choice — exerts the most powerful and enduring influence on a character's life. Family relationships cannot be "deleted" like a social media friend; they can only be maintained, strained, or estranged.

Family Roles and Mechanics 👨‍👩‍👧‍👦

Family Role Default Tier Mechanical Impact
👨‍👩‍👧 Parents/Guardians Family-Level (if close) or Close Friend (if distant) Provide financial support, emotional support, housing. Expect regular contact. Can enable or undermine life choices.
👫 Siblings Friend to Close Friend (based on relationship quality) Shared history creates automatic connection points. Can be allies or rivals. Sibling rivalry adds +1 difficulty to Communication checks between them.
👶 Children Family-Level (automatic) Massive time and financial investment. +3 to Mental Health when healthy, but −3 to free time, savings rate, and sleep. Add 2–4 hours daily of care obligations.
👵 Extended Family Acquaintance to Friend Expected at gatherings. Can provide networking, support, or obligations. +1 to Social Capital in your cultural community for maintaining ties.

Family Obligations 📋

Family ties create mechanical obligations — things your character is expected to do, whether they want to or not:

  • 🎄 Holidays and gatherings: Expected attendance. Missing one costs 1–3 relationship points per absent event. Attending costs a day of time and $50–200.
  • 💰 Financial support: Family members may request money. Refusing costs relationship points; accepting costs actual money.
  • 🫂 Emotional labor: Being "the strong one" costs 1 Stress per week of sustained emotional support for others.
  • 🏥 Caregiving: A sick or aging parent may require 2–10 hours per week of caregiving, reducing available time.

Estrangement Mechanics 🚪

Family estrangement is the most severe relationship state. It is irreversible without significant narrative work:

  • Causes: Abuse (emotional, physical, financial), betrayal, irreconcilable values, chronic disappointment, or a single devastating event
  • Mechanical effect: The estranged person is treated as a Stranger. All relationship points frozen at 0. No new points for at minimum 1 year of game time.
  • Mental health impact: Estrangement causes ongoing Stress — 1 per week if forced upon the character, 0 if the character chose it.
  • Reconciliation: Requires a Very Hard Communication check (10+) AND a Strong Vulnerability check (9+). Both must succeed. Even then, rebuilds only from Acquaintance level.
📖 Scenario: The Family Dinner
Diego
At his family's holiday dinner — exhausted but committed
Stress: 4/10 Father: 22 pts (Close Friend) Cost: $80 + 6 hrs

Diego is invited to his family's annual holiday dinner. His father (Close Friend tier, 22 points) expects him to attend. Diego has been working double shifts and is exhausted — attending would cost him $80 (gift + travel) and 6 hours. If he goes: gains +3 relationship points with his father, but loses the evening's sleep (−1 to next day's Physical checks). If he doesn't go: loses 3 relationship points with his father AND his mother. Diego rolls a 1d6 on his Stress — he's at 4/10, so he's not in crisis. He decides to go. The dinner is pleasant but his father makes a comment about Diego still not being "successful" — Diego rolls an Empathy check (fails) and takes it as a personal attack rather than his father's own insecurity manifesting. Diego gains +2 relationship points (net gain of −1 from the comment), leaving him at 21. Close call.

Gaining Social Capital 📈

  • 🤲 Helping others: +1 per meaningful act of kindness (volunteering, helping a neighbor, mentoring)
  • 🏘️ Community participation: +1 per month of active participation in community events
  • 🎖️ Professional achievement: +2 for a promotion, published work, or recognized accomplishment
  • 💝 Generosity: +1 per significant act of generosity (hosting, donating, lending)

Losing Social Capital 📉

  • 💥 Conflict in public: −2 per public argument or scene
  • ❌ Broken promises: −1 per broken commitment
  • 🗣️ Gossip: −1 to −3 per damaging rumor that spreads (see below)
  • ⚖️ Legal trouble: −3 to −5 per arrest, conviction, or public legal proceeding
  • ⏰ Being unreliable: −1 per instance of flaking on plans or being consistently late

Gossip Mechanics 💬

Gossip is a slow-moving but powerful force. When something noteworthy happens to a character, it spreads through their social network:

  • 📅 Day 1–3: Only close contacts know (Close Friends and above)
  • 📅 Day 4–14: Spreads to Friends tier and wider acquaintance network
  • 📅 Day 15–30: Community-wide awareness. Affects Social Capital.
  • 📅 Day 31+: Becomes part of the character's reputation — a lasting modifier to how strangers initially treat them
💡 Spending Social Capital

A character can spend Social Capital to gain advantages: −1 to a Communication check (spend 1), get a favor from someone outside their network (spend 2), receive preferential treatment in a bureaucratic process (spend 3), or have someone vouch for them in a professional setting (spend 2). Social Capital cannot go below 0.

📖 Scenario: The Gossip Spiral
Nina
After a public argument at the office party — Social Capital spiraling down
SC: 12 → 10 → 8 → 5 Status: Respected → Known → Unknown

Nina gets into a public argument with a coworker at the office holiday party (Escalation Level 4). Word spreads quickly. By Monday morning, half the office knows. Nina loses 2 Social Capital in her workplace community (from 12 to 10 — drops from "Respected" to "Known"). Her boss asks her to "be more professional." Over the next month, the story mutates — people say Nina "started crying and threw a drink" (she didn't). Nina loses another 2 Social Capital (now at 8). She spends 3 Social Capital to have her manager send a clarifying email to the team, bringing her back to 5 — barely out of "Unknown" territory. It will take months of consistent good behavior to rebuild.

7. Networking and Professional Relationships 💼

Your professional network is one of the most valuable resources in modern life. It affects career advancement, job opportunities, and even personal well-being.

Professional Contact Types 📇

Contact Type How You Meet Mechanical Benefit
👔 Colleague Work alongside each other Information sharing, cover for you when absent, reference for future jobs
🧭 Mentor Experienced person who guides you (formal or informal) −1 to all career-related checks while active. Can advocate for promotions. Prevents costly mistakes.
🎓 Mentee Someone you're guiding Builds professional reputation (+1 Social Capital per quarter). Future mentees may become powerful contacts.
🌐 Industry Contact Met through conferences, LinkedIn, professional groups Job leads, industry information, referral opportunities. 1d6 per quarter for a potential job opportunity (TN 45).
📋 Former Boss Previously worked under them Can provide a reference (+2 to interview checks if positive). If negative, can block opportunities (−2).
⚔️ Competitor Works in the same field/level Can be ally or obstacle. Roll 1d6 per major career decision — on a 1, they actively work against you.

Maintaining Professional Networks 🔗

Professional relationships require active maintenance, just like personal ones:

  • 📞 Monthly: Reach out to at least 2 contacts (coffee, call, LinkedIn message). Failure to maintain = contact degrades one tier per quarter of neglect.
  • 🎪 Quarterly: Attend 1 networking event (industry meetup, conference, happy hour). Costs $20–100 and 2–4 hours. Provides +1 Social Capital in your professional community.
  • 📄 Annually: Update your professional presence (resume, LinkedIn, portfolio). Costs time but prevents −1 Social Capital for appearing "out of touch."

How Contacts Help (or Hinder) 🤲

When a character needs professional help, the GM checks their contact list:

  • 🔥 Warm introduction: A contact introduces you to someone they know. 1d6 — on 4+, the introduction is positive and you gain an Acquaintance with the new person.
  • 💼 Job referral: A contact puts your name forward for a position. 1d6 — on 5+, you get an interview. The contact's Social Capital in their company affects the roll (+1 per tier above 10).
  • 📝 Reference: A contact vouches for your character. Adds +1 to +3 (based on relationship tier) to the relevant check.
  • 🔥 Burned bridge: If a character left a job poorly, that contact can actively block opportunities. 1d6 — on 1–2, the contact speaks negatively, causing −2 to the relevant check.
📖 Scenario: The Mentor's Call
Raj
Applying for a lead role with mentor Patricia's advocacy
Mentor: Patricia (Close Friend) Interview: 8 + mods = 11 ✓

Raj has been mentored by Patricia, a senior engineer at his company, for 18 months (Mentor relationship, Close Friend tier). Raj wants to apply for a lead role that just opened. Patricia agrees to advocate for him. This gives Raj −1 to his interview check (easier target) AND +2 from her reference. Raj rolls 2d10 for the interview: gets an 8. With modifiers, that's effectively a 11 — a strong success. Raj gets the promotion. Patricia gains +1 Social Capital in the engineering community for developing talent. Raj gains +2 Social Capital for the promotion itself.

8. Community and Belonging 🏘️

Humans are social creatures. Belonging to a community is a fundamental need — and in Reality RPG, the absence of community has real mechanical consequences.

Community Types 🏛️

Community Entry Requirement Benefits Cost
🏘️ Neighborhood Live there Local knowledge, neighborly help, safety in numbers. +1 to Social Capital per quarter of active participation. HOA fees, neighborhood expectations, gossip exposure
⛪ Religious/Spiritual Attend services or participate +2 to Mental Health checks per month of attendance. Strong support network. +1 Social Capital within that community. Time commitment (2–4 hours/week), possible financial contributions, doctrinal expectations
🎨 Hobby/Interest Group Share the interest; attend meetings +1 to Mental Health checks per month of participation. Skill improvement in the hobby. Social connections with shared interests. Time commitment, equipment costs, possible membership fees
🏛️ Civic/Volunteer Sign up; show up +2 Social Capital in the wider community. +1 to Mental Health checks. Networking opportunities. Resume building. Significant time commitment (4–10 hours/week depending on role)
🌍 Cultural/Ethnic Shared heritage or identity +2 to Mental Health checks (sense of belonging). Cultural support during holidays and crises. +1 Social Capital within the cultural community. Expectations around cultural participation; possible pressure to conform
💻 Online Community Join forums, Discord, social media groups +1 to Mental Health checks per month of active participation. Information sharing, niche connections. Lower time cost. Less deep connection (cannot reach Friend tier in person). Risk of echo chambers and online conflict.

Isolation Mechanics 🏝️

🔴 The Isolation Threshold

If a character has no community membership AND fewer than 3 relationships above Acquaintance tier, they are considered isolated. Isolation imposes the following penalties:

  • −2 to all Mental Health checks (ongoing)
  • +1 Stress per week (loneliness, lack of support)
  • −1 to Communication checks (social skills atrophy without practice)
  • No Social Capital gains — without community, there's no one to build reputation with
  • Job search penalty: +1 difficulty on all job-related checks (most jobs found through networks)

Isolation is compounding — the longer a character remains isolated, the harder it becomes to break out. After 3 months of isolation, all Mental Health penalties increase by 1. After 6 months, increase by 1 more.

Breaking Isolation 🔓

Rebuilding community takes effort and time. A character who wants to escape isolation must:

  1. Take an initiative action: Join a class, attend a community event, volunteer, or reach out to someone. Requires a Resolve check (2d10 + Resolve, TN 50) if deeply isolated (3+ months).
  2. Attend consistently: Showing up once doesn't count. 3+ attendances within a month establishes a foothold.
  3. Build a connection: Within the new community, form at least 1 Acquaintance-level relationship within 2 months.
📖 Scenario: Breaking the Isolation
Tom, 42
Recently isolated — joining a running club to rebuild connections
Isolated: 2 weeks Resolve check: 10 ✓ New contact: Keisha

Tom, 42, recently moved to a new city for work. His old friends are 3 states away (relationship points decaying). He has no local community, no Close Friends, and only 1 Acquaintance (his new coworker). Tom is isolated. He gains +1 Stress per week and has −2 to Mental Health checks. After 2 weeks, his Mental Health check to handle his morning routine fails — he's struggling.

Tom decides to join a local running club. Resolve check: 2d10 (8) + Resolve (2) = 10. Success — he shows up. The first meeting is awkward (Communication check fails — he doesn't know anyone). But he shows up again the next week, and the week after that. On the third visit, a regular named Keisha notices him and introduces herself. They bond over their mutual struggle with hill training. Tom gains his first local Acquaintance. The isolation penalties begin to lift — the +1 Stress per week stops. It will take months to fully rebuild, but the dam has cracked.

9. Discrimination and Microaggressions 🏳️‍🌈

Discrimination is not a "choice" in Reality RPG — it is a structural reality that affects characters differently based on their race, gender, sexuality, disability, religion, class, age, and other identity factors. The game models this not as individual villainy but as systemic friction that certain characters navigate daily.

3–4 ⚠️ Subtle discrimination: Microaggression, implicit bias, differential treatment A resume with a "sounding ethnic" name gets fewer callbacks; an older worker is excluded from "team building" drinks; a disabled person's needs treated as an inconvenience
5 ❓ Ambiguous situation: Could be bias, could be coincidence. Character must decide how to interpret A security guard follows a person of color in a store; a manager doesn't promote someone — was it bias or qualifications?
6 ✅ No discrimination: The situation proceeds without bias The interaction is neutral or positive

Frequency modifier: The GM adjusts the Discrimination Check frequency based on the character's identity and the setting. A Black man walking alone at night in a predominantly white suburb faces discrimination checks every scene. A white man in the same situation might face them once per month. The GM uses their knowledge of real-world statistics and the player's described identity to set frequency.

Responding to Discrimination ⚖️

When discrimination occurs, the character has choices — each with consequences:

Response Check Required Consequences
📢 Stand Up — confront the bias directly 2d10 + Resolve + Communication, TN 55 Success: Biased person backs down; bystanders may support. +1 Social Capital among allies. Failure: Escalation; retaliation. +2 Stress regardless.
🤐 Stay Quiet — swallow the discomfort and move on None (automatic) No immediate escalation. +1 Stress (suppressed anger). −1 to next Mental Health check (internalized harm).
📝 Document/Report — file a complaint, record evidence 2d10 + Expression, TN 50 Success: Incident formally recorded. May lead to institutional change. +1 Social Capital. Failure: Dismissed or backfires. +2 Stress.
🫂 Seek Support — tell a trusted person, process it None (automatic if you have the relationship) Stress is halved (rounded down) if you have a Close Friend or above to talk to. +1 relationship point with the confidant.

Allyship Mechanics 🤝

Characters who are not directly affected by a given form of discrimination can still play a crucial role as allies:

  • 🛡️ Bystander intervention: When someone else faces discrimination, an ally can step in. Roll 2d10 + Courage + Communication, TN 50. Success de-escalates. Affected person gains +1 relationship point with the ally.
  • 📢 Amplification: In group settings, an ally can amplify marginalized voices. The ally takes a −1 penalty to their own Communication check to give the marginalized person a +2 bonus on theirs.
  • 🏛️ Institutional allyship: An ally in power can use privilege to change policies, hire diversely, or challenge systemic bias. Requires 2d10 + Social Capital (as a numeric bonus), TN 55. Creates a lasting positive modifier for affected characters.

Cumulative Impact ⚡

The real damage of discrimination is cumulative. The GM tracks a character's Bias Exposure Score — the total number of discrimination encounters they've faced (whether they responded or not):

😐 0–5 encounters — No lasting mechanical effect beyond individual incident consequences
😟 6–15 encounters — −1 to Communication checks in settings where the bias originates (hypervigilance, exhaustion)
😰 16–30 encounters — −2 to Mental Health checks in those settings. Character develops coping mechanisms
🚨 31+ encounters — −3 to all checks in those settings. May develop a trauma response. GM should check in with player
💡 GM Guidance

Discrimination scenes can be emotionally heavy for players, especially when the bias matches their real-world identity. Always check in before playing these scenes. Use the X-card. Offer to fade to black. The mechanical system exists to honor the reality of discrimination — not to re-traumatize. If a player says "let's skip this," skip it. The mechanical penalties can be applied narratively without playing the scene.

📖 Scenario: The Interview
Amara
Interviewing for a corporate finance role — facing subtle discrimination
Discrimination roll: 3 (subtle) Communication: 14 ✓ Bias Score: 12

Amara, a Black woman interviewing for a corporate finance role, faces a Discrimination Check from the GM. He rolls 1d6: gets a 3 — subtle discrimination. During the interview, the interviewers ask Amara if she can "code-switch" and seem surprised when she mentions her church community. They don't ask the white candidate these questions.

Amara's player chooses Stay Quiet — she answers professionally but internally burns with frustration. +1 Stress, −1 to her next Mental Health check. However, Amara's strong Communication skill carries her through the interview itself — she rolls 2d10 (10) + Communication (4) = 14. She gets the job despite the bias. Her Bias Exposure Score increases to 12 — she's now at the threshold where she'll gain a −1 to Communication checks in corporate settings (the weight of knowing this happens every time).

Later, Amara's coworker David witnesses a similar incident with a new hire. He chooses Bystander Intervention, rolling 2d10 (9) + Courage (2) + Communication (3) = 14. He gently redirects the conversation. The new hire gains +1 relationship point with David, and the team culture begins to shift.

Quick Reference: Social Stress Effects 📊

📋 Social Stress Tracker

Social interactions generate Stress just like physical danger does. Track cumulative Stress from:

💥 Arguments & Conflicts
1–5 per incident
❌ Failed Comm. Checks
1 per failure
🚧 Discrimination
1–2 per encounter
🏝️ Isolation
1 per week
🫂 Family Obligations
1 per week
💔 Breakups
3–8 per incident

Stress is reduced through positive social interaction (−1 per meaningful hangout), community participation (−1 per event), sleep (−1 per full night's rest), and professional help (−2 per therapy session).

⚔️ Fantasy vs. Reality: Social Mechanics

🐉 Fantasy RPG

  • "Persuade" skill check to talk your way out of anything
  • Charisma dump stat or god stat — binary
  • Relationships are flavor text
  • One failed roll = NPC hates you forever
  • No consequences for social actions

🏙️ Reality RPG

  • Multiple skills (Communication, Empathy, Deception) with nuanced outcomes
  • Relationships are a tracked, decaying resource with real mechanical weight
  • Every social interaction has consequences — positive and negative
  • Social capital compounds over time like experience points
  • Discrimination, gossip, and systemic bias are modeled
