📖 Glossary of Mechanical Terms

Definitions for every mechanical term used across the Reality RPG ruleset. Use this page as a quick reference when a term is unclear or you need to verify a formula.

A — Attributes & Modifiers

VIG — Vitality

The physical health and stamina attribute. Governs HP (Hit Points), resistance to illness and injury, and physical endurance. High VIG means your character can push through exhaustion and recover from physical trauma more easily.

Where used: Health Condition Track, exercise checks, physical labor, carrying loads.

Source: Core Mechanics

INT — Intelligence

The cognitive processing and knowledge attribute. Governs learning, analysis, problem-solving, and academic performance. High INT means your character grasps complex concepts quickly and makes informed decisions.

Where used: Academic checks, research, troubleshooting, shopping bargains.

Source: Core Mechanics

EXP — Experience

The life experience and worldly wisdom attribute. Governs practical judgment, social awareness, financial literacy, and navigating bureaucratic systems. High EXP means your character has learned from past mistakes and can read situations well.

Where used: Financial management, career advancement, insurance claims, legal matters.

Source: Core Mechanics

DXT — Dexterity

The physical coordination and fine motor skill attribute. Governs hand-eye coordination, manual tasks, driving, sports performance, and item maintenance. High DXT means your character is nimble and precise with their hands.

Where used: Driving checks, item durability maintenance, sports, cooking, crafts.

Source: Core Mechanics

RES — Resilience

The mental toughness and emotional stability attribute. Governs Stress Point maximum, resistance to psychological trauma, and recovery from mental health episodes. High RES means your character can withstand more stress before breaking down.

SP Max formula: 20 + (RES mod) × 5

Source: Core Mechanics, Mental Health

PRC — Perception

The awareness and social sensitivity attribute. Governs noticing details, reading body language, sensing danger, and cultural awareness. High PRC means your character picks up on subtle social cues and environmental changes.

Where used: Social awareness, threat detection, cultural navigation, safety checks.

Source: Core Mechanics

Attribute Modifier

The bonus derived from an attribute score, used in all dice rolls. Calculated as:

Mod = floor(attr ÷ 5)

Example: VIG 12 → floor(12 ÷ 5) = floor(2.4) = +2

Example: RES 7 → floor(7 ÷ 5) = floor(1.4) = +1

Source: Core Mechanics

C — Core Resolution

2d10 System

The core dice resolution mechanic. Roll two d10s and sum them (range 2–20, bell curve distribution), add the relevant attribute modifier, and compare to a Target Number (TN).

Lower rolls are better: roll + modifier ≤ TN = success.

The bell curve means moderate results (8–13) are most common. Extreme results (2–4 or 17–20) are rare.

Source: Core Mechanics §1

TN — Target Number

The difficulty threshold for a check. Your total roll (2d10 + modifier) must be ≤ TN to succeed. The standard TN scale is:

TNDifficulty
25Trivial
35Easy
50Medium
65Hard
80Very Hard
90Near Impossible

Source: Core Mechanics §7

Critical Success

When the raw dice roll (before modifiers) is ≤ 5, the outcome is an extraordinary, almost lucky success. The action succeeds with exceptional benefit beyond normal success.

Source: Core Mechanics §1

Critical Failure

When the raw dice roll (before modifiers) is ≥ 96 (on d100 scale) or ≥ 19 (on 2d10 summed scale), the outcome is a catastrophic, almost unlucky failure. The action fails with additional negative consequences.

Source: Core Mechanics §1

Strong Success

When roll + modifier ≤ TN − 5. The action succeeds exceptionally well, often granting bonus benefits (extra SP recovery, faster progress, better outcomes).

Source: Core Mechanics §1

Partial Success

When roll + modifier ≤ TN + 5 (but > TN). The action succeeds but with a cost, complication, or reduced benefit.

Source: Core Mechanics §1

H — Health & Stress

SP — Stress Points

The resource tracking mental and emotional strain. SP accumulates from stressful events and depletes through recovery activities. When SP reaches Max SP, the character enters an Episode.

Max SP formula: 20 + (RES mod) × 5

  • RES mod +0 (RES 1–4): Max SP = 20
  • RES mod +1 (RES 5–9): Max SP = 25
  • RES mod +2 (RES 10–14): Max SP = 30
  • RES mod +3 (RES 15–19): Max SP = 35
  • RES mod +4 (RES 20): Max SP = 40

Source: Core Mechanics §4, Mental Health

Stress Levels

Five tiers of stress intensity, determined by current SP relative to Max SP. Thresholds vary by RES modifier:

LevelDescription
StableSP at 0. Character is at baseline.
ElevatedLow SP. Minor stress, manageable.
StressedModerate SP. Noticeable strain on daily life.
OverwhelmedHigh SP. Functioning is impaired.
EpisodeSP = Max SP. Full breakdown; character cannot function normally.

Source: Core Mechanics §4, Mental Health

Episode

A mental health crisis triggered when SP reaches Max SP. During an Episode, all checks suffer a +10 TN penalty. Episodes last 1d4 hours or days depending on severity. After an Episode ends, SP resets to 50% of Max SP.

Source: Mental Health

Trigger

A specific event or stimulus that causes an immediate SP gain, potentially pushing a character toward or into an Episode. Triggers are personalized based on character backstory, mental health conditions, and past trauma.

Source: Mental Health

Health Condition Track

The physical health progression system. Six states from best to worst:

StateModifierDescription
Healthy—Full physical capacity.
Tired−5 to physical checksFatigue setting in.
Sick−10 to physical checksIllness or minor injury.
Injured−15 to physical checksSignificant physical trauma.
Critical−20 to all checksLife-threatening condition.
Dead—Character has died.

Source: Core Mechanics §3

R — Recovery

SP Recovery (Rest)

−1 SP per night's sleep. A good night's rest reduces stress by 1 point. This is the baseline recovery rate available to all characters.

Source: Core Mechanics §4

SP Recovery (Social Support)

−2 SP per meaningful social interaction. A genuine conversation with a friend, partner, or support person reduces stress by 2 points.

Source: Core Mechanics §4

SP Recovery (Exercise)

−1 SP per workout (−2 SP on strong success). Physical exercise provides modest stress relief but cannot replace therapy for deep recovery.

Source: Core Mechanics §4, Exercise

SP Recovery (Therapy)

−3 to −5 SP per therapy session. The most effective SP recovery method. The exact amount depends on therapy type, therapist skill, and character engagement.

  • Individual therapy: −3 SP per session (−5 on strong success)
  • Group therapy: −2 to −3 SP per session
  • Intensive therapy (IBLT, residential): −5 SP per session

Source: Core Mechanics §4, Mental Health

Recovery Stage

The phase of mental health recovery a character is in. Stages progress from Acute → Stabilizing → Recovering → Maintained. Each stage has different SP gain/loss modifiers and available treatment options.

Source: Mental Health

Therapy Level

The intensity tier of mental health treatment. Levels range from self-care (Level 0) through clinical therapy (Level 1–2) to intensive residential programs (Level 3). Higher levels provide greater SP recovery but require more time and resources.

Source: Mental Health

E — Economy & Inventory

Mood Score

A derived stat reflecting current emotional state. Calculated as 10 − current_stress_level where stress levels are numbered 0 (Stable) through 4 (Episode). At Mood Score ≤ 5, shopping and financial checks suffer a +5 TN penalty.

Source: Inventory & Economy

Budget Score

A measure of financial stability. Calculated as INT mod + EXP mod. Tiers:

  • 0–2 (Tight): +10 TN to all financial checks. Barely making ends meet.
  • 3–4 (Comfortable): No modifiers. Managing finances adequately.
  • 5+ (Well-funded): −5 TN to financial checks. Financial cushion available.

Source: Inventory & Economy

Overdraft Risk

A mechanical state entered when spending exceeds the character's Budget Score tier. While in Overdraft Risk, the character must roll 2d10 + EXP mod vs TN 50 each week; on failure, gain 2 SP from financial stress.

Source: Inventory & Economy

Durability Score

Items have a Durability Score representing their condition. Based on cost tier: Cheap = 3, Standard = 5, Premium = 8, Luxury = 10. Items lose Durability through use and damage. When Durability reaches 0, the item is broken or lost. Repair requires 2d10 + DXT mod vs TN 50 + (original − current) × 5.

Source: Inventory & Economy

Encumbrance

The weight-carrying system. Comfortable limit = VIG mod × 10 kg. Exceeding by 1× adds +10 TN to all physical checks. Exceeding by 2× adds +20 TN and forces a 2d10 + VIG mod vs TN 50 check each hour or gain 1 SP.

Source: Inventory & Economy

S — Social

Social Capital

A point pool (0–15+) representing the character's social standing and network strength. Levels: Outcast (0–2), Marginalized (3–5), Integrated (6–8), Connected (9–11), Influential (12–14), Legendary (15+).

Numeric bonus for dice checks: 1 bonus point per 3 Social Capital points, capped at +5. (Outcast = +0, Marginalized = +1, Integrated = +2, Connected = +3, Influential = +4, Legendary = +5)

Source: Social Dynamics
