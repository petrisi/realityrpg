🛠️ GM Tools

Your command center for running Reality RPG. Random tables, generators, trackers, reference cards, and ethical guidelines — everything you need to run a session without opening another tab.

📖 Quick Reference

Core resolution: 2d10 + modifier vs TN. Max SP (Stress Points) = 20 + RES mod × 5. Attributes: VIG (Vigor), INT (Intelligence), EXP (Experience), DXT (Dexterity), RES (Resilience), PRC (Presence). TN ranges: 8 (easy) to 16 (nearly impossible). See Core Mechanics for full details.

📊 4. Economic Climate Tracker

The broader economy affects every character. Track these four indicators at the start of each campaign and adjust them quarterly (every 3 sessions). Roll d6 for each — 1–2 = worse, 3–4 = stable, 5–6 = better.

Indicator🔴 Bad (1–2)🟡 Stable (3–4)🟢 Good (5–6)Effect on PCs
🏠 Housing Market Rents rising fast; vacancies low; landlords aggressive Normal rental market; some options available Rents stable or dropping; more inventory Bad: +1 TN on housing checks; +1 SP. Good: −1 TN on housing checks; −1 SP.
💼 Job Market Layoffs; hiring freezes; wage suppression Normal hiring; competitive but fair Job boom; wage increases; more openings Bad: +1 TN on employment checks; +1 SP. Good: −1 TN on employment checks.
🏥 Healthcare Access Insurance gaps; long wait times; rising costs Adequate access; normal costs Expanded coverage; shorter waits; lower costs Bad: +1 TN on health checks; +1 SP. Good: −1 TN on health checks.
🌍 Social Climate Polarization; discrimination; social tension Normal social friction; manageable Community cohesion; social support strong Bad: +1 TN on social checks; +1 SP. Good: −1 TN on social checks; −1 SP.
📈 Economic Climate Rules

Starting state: Roll all four indicators at campaign start. Quarterly shift: Every 3 sessions, roll d6 for each indicator. If the result differs by 2+ from the current state, shift it one step. If all four indicators are 🔴 for two consecutive quarters, trigger a macro crisis event (recession, pandemic, natural disaster) — see Macro Economy. If all four are 🟢, trigger a golden period — bonus SP recovery for all PCs.

📇 6. Quick Reference Cards

Print these for your table. Each card covers a core mechanic at a glance.

🎲 Card 1: Core Resolution

Roll:2d10 + Attribute Modifier vs Target Number (TN)
Attributes:VIG (Vigor), INT (Intelligence), EXP (Experience), DXT (Dexterity), RES (Resilience), PRC (Presence)
TN Guide:8 = Trivial · 10 = Easy · 12 = Moderate · 14 = Hard · 16 = Very Hard · 18 = Nearly Impossible
Success:Result ≥ TN = success. 5+ over TN = exceptional success (GM may award bonus).
Failure:Result < TN = failure. 5+ below TN = critical failure (complications arise).
Reality RPG — Core Resolution Reference

💚 Card 2: Stress Points (SP)

Max SP:20 + (RES modifier × 5)
Example:RES 8 (mod −2) → Max SP = 20 + (−10) = 10. RES 12 (mod +2) → Max SP = 20 + 10 = 30.
Gaining SP:Stressful events add SP. See event tables for specific amounts.
Recovery:1 SP/week downtime · 3 SP/week genuine rest · 5 SP/week vacation or therapy
SP Overload:Exceeding max SP triggers a stress event — panic attack, breakdown, illness. See Mental Health.
SP = 0:Character is at peace. Rare and precious. All checks gain +1 bonus.

⚖️ 8. GM Ethics Guide

Reality RPG deals with real human struggles — poverty, illness, discrimination, grief, trauma, and everything in between. Running this game responsibly is not optional. It is the foundation of everything.

🛡️ Core Principle

The game is about reality, not exploitation. Difficult topics exist in the game because they exist in life. The GM's job is to handle them with care, respect, and intentionality — never for shock value, drama, or "realism" at the expense of player wellbeing.

🔒 Before Play Begins

StepWhat to DoWhy
1. Lines & Veils Every player lists topics that are strictly off-limits (lines) and topics that can occur but should be faded to black (veils). No exceptions. No negotiation. Players have different trauma histories. What's fine for one person is devastating for another. This is non-negotiable.
2. X-Card System Agree on a signal (physical card, word, hand gesture) that any player can use at any time to stop content immediately. No explanation required. No debate. Just stop. People can't always predict what will trigger them. The X-card is a safety net that must be absolutely trusted.
3. Content Warnings Give advance notice before any session that will deal with heavy topics: "Next session involves a health diagnosis scene." Allow players to prepare or opt out. Surprise is great for plot twists. Surprise is terrible for triggering trauma. Warn players.
4. Check-ins Start each session with a quick emotional check-in: "How are you doing today? Is there anything you need me to know before we start?" Players' real lives affect their play. A bad day at work, a personal loss, or stress from outside the game changes what they can handle.
5. Aftercare End heavy sessions with a debrief: "That was intense. How is everyone? Want to talk about it?" Offer a lighter activity or check-in before ending. Players can get emotionally absorbed. Help them transition back to their real lives.

⚠️ During Play

🚫 Things the GM Should Never Do
  • Never use a player's real-life trauma as plot material without explicit, enthusiastic consent.
  • Never force a player to roleplay a scene they've marked as a line.
  • Never dismiss or minimize a player's emotional response to game content.
  • Never use suffering as "character development" without the player's buy-in.
  • Never make real-world marginalized experiences into "challenges to overcome" for entertainment.
  • Never pressure a player to continue if they've used the X-card or asked for a break.
✅ Things the GM Should Always Do
  • Always check in with players before introducing heavy content.
  • Always give players agency over their character's emotional responses.
  • Always honor the X-card immediately and without question.
  • Always balance difficulty with hope — every dark scene should have a path forward.
  • Always remind players this is a game and it's okay to step away.
  • Always debrief after intense sessions.
  • Always respect that a player's "no" is final, for any reason, at any time.

🧭 Running Sensitive Topics

TopicApproachSafety Tool
Mental illness Pull from the Mental Health mechanics. Treat conditions with accuracy and respect. Recovery is possible but not guaranteed. Lines & veils for specific conditions. X-card for triggers. Content warning 24 hours in advance.
Trauma & abuse Fade to black. Never describe the act. Focus on aftermath, processing, and healing. The player controls what happened to their character. Strict line on description. Veil on all references. Player owns the backstory entirely.
Discrimination & racism If included, it should be at the request of a player whose character has this experience. Research real impacts. Never use as "spice." Player consent required. Content warning mandatory. Aftercare essential.
Death & grief Use the Death & Grief framework. Grief is real and messy. Allow players to control the pace of grief scenes. Content warning. Check-ins before and after. Allow players to skip grief scenes.
Poverty & financial stress Ground in real mechanics (Inventory & Economy). Show both the struggle and the community support systems. Avoid poverty porn. Player consent for poverty scenarios. Balance with moments of relief and community.
Sexual health & intimacy Use the Sexual Health framework. Fade to black on all intimate scenes. Focus on consent, communication, and health. Strict lines on description. Player consent for any romantic content. X-card always active.
Disability Use the Disability framework. Disability is a life experience, not a penalty. Consult real disability resources and communities. Player consent for disability scenarios. Respect disability identity. Never use as punishment.
Addiction & substance use If included, treat with clinical accuracy. Recovery is possible. Relapse is part of recovery for some. Never glamorize. Strict content warning. Lines on specific substances. Player owns their character's relationship to substances.

🌱 The GM's Own Wellbeing

💚 Take Care of Yourself Too

Running Reality RPG means carrying the emotional weight of the game for your players. That's real labor.

  • Debrief yourself after heavy sessions. Journal, talk to someone, take a walk.
  • Set boundaries on how much emotional labor you can handle in one session.
  • It's okay to say no to a player's request for a scenario you're not comfortable running.
  • Rotate GM duties if your group is large enough. Shared responsibility is sustainable responsibility.
  • Remember: You are facilitating a game, not providing therapy. If a player needs professional support, gently suggest they seek it.
