Daily Life

Sleep, hygiene, food, commuting, working, chores, leisure, and aging — the routines that sustain a human being and what happens when they break down.

🌅 Daily Life Mechanics

In Reality RPG, every character must survive the grind of ordinary existence. This page covers the full Daily Life system: how characters sleep, eat, work, commute, clean, relax, and age. These are not optional side quests — they are the core gameplay loop. A character who neglects these systems will deteriorate, fail at their job, lose relationships, and eventually collapse under the weight of their own neglect.

⚖️ Core Rule: Daily Life Is Mandatory

Unlike fantasy RPGs where characters can skip meals, sleep under a tree, and ignore personal hygiene without consequence, Reality RPG tracks all daily needs. Every day, the GM asks each player to account for how their character met (or failed to meet) their basic needs. Neglect has cumulative, mechanical penalties that affect every aspect of play.

📋 The Daily Life Checklist

Each day, a character must manage the following systems. The GM can resolve these at a high level during mundane stretches ("You slept, ate, went to work, came home, washed dishes, and watched TV — nothing notable happened") or drill down when circumstances make them interesting.

💡 GM Guidance: When to Detail Daily Life

Don't bog down every session tracking every shower and sandwich. Use narrative compression during stable periods: "Three weeks pass. You work your shifts, eat, sleep, pay rent. Your savings drop by $800." Zoom in when something goes wrong — a broken water heater, a food poisoning incident, a missed alarm causing a late arrival at work.

😴 §1. Sleep System

Sleep is the most fundamental daily need. Without it, everything else degrades. In Reality RPG, sleep is tracked on a Sleep Need Track that measures both hours slept and sleep quality.

⚠️ Warning: Sleep Debt Is Deadly

After 11 days without sleep, a human being can die. In game terms, if a character reaches 11 consecutive days of 0 sleep (not just deficient sleep, but zero sleep), they suffer a fatal health event. The GM should make this clear: sleep deprivation is a genuine, escalating threat.

1.2 Sleep Quality Factors

Sleeping enough hours isn't enough — the quality matters. At the end of each night, determine sleep quality by rolling 2d10 + VIG modifier against a TN of 40, modified by environmental factors:

📝 Example: Maya's Sleep Quality

Maya (VIG 12, modifier +2) sleeps in her own bed (+10) in a quiet apartment (+10), but she had an iced latte at 5 PM (−15), checked her phone until 11:30 (−5), and is stressed about a work deadline (SP = 14, so −4). Her total modifier is +2. She rolls 2d10 = 47. Total: 47 + 2 = 49 against TN 40. This is a failure — her sleep is poor quality. She gets 7 hours but wakes up feeling like 4. The GM applies a −1 penalty to all her rolls tomorrow as if she had 1 day of sleep debt, even though she technically slept enough hours.

VIG 12 (+2) SP 14 Roll: 49 vs TN 40 ❌

1.3 Sleep Quality Results

🌟 Critical Success (≤ 5 before modifiers) — Deep, restorative sleep. Gain +1 VIG (up to max) and clear 1 day of Sleep Debt if any.
✅ Strong Success (≤ TN − 5) — Excellent sleep. Full VIG recovery. No sleep debt accumulates.
✅ Success (≤ TN) — Adequate sleep. Normal recovery. No sleep debt accumulates.
⚠️ Partial Success (≤ TN + 5) — Marginal sleep. Half VIG recovery. Counts as 0.5 day of sleep debt.
❌ Failure — Poor sleep. No VIG recovery. Counts as 1 day of sleep debt regardless of hours in bed.
💀 Critical Failure (≥ 96 before modifiers) — Terrifying nightmare or complete sleep disruption. Lose 1 VIG. Counts as 2 days of sleep debt. May trigger a Stress Event.

1.4 Insomnia Mechanics

Some characters suffer from clinical insomnia — an inability to fall or stay asleep despite having the opportunity. This is a persistent condition that affects the Sleep System:

😟

Situational Insomnia

Once per week, the character cannot fall asleep for at least 3 hours. TN for sleep quality is +10. Mild — manageable with wind-down routines.

😰

Persistent Insomnia

2–3 nights per week, sleep onset takes 2+ hours. Sleep quality TN is +15. Cumulative sleep debt builds quickly. Moderate — requires treatment or coping strategies.

😱

Chronic Insomnia

Nearly every night. Sleep quality TN is +25. Character is almost always accumulating sleep debt. Must make a 2d10 + MEDICINE check each week (TN 50) or suffer a health event. Severe — needs professional intervention.

⚖️ Treating Insomnia

Characters can attempt to manage insomnia through:

  • Over-the-counter sleep aids (e.g., melatonin): +5 to sleep quality TN, but causes next-day grogginess (−1 to all rolls the following morning)
  • Prescription sleep medication: +15 to sleep quality TN, but carries a dependency risk — each week of use requires a 2d10 roll (TN 60). On failure, the character develops tolerance and needs higher doses.
  • Cognitive Behavioral Therapy for Insomnia (CBT-I): Over 8–12 weeks of therapy sessions, the character can reduce their insomnia severity by one level (Chronic → Persistent → Situational → resolved). Each session costs $100–$200 out of pocket or requires insurance.
  • Lifestyle changes: Consistent sleep schedule (+5), no caffeine after 2 PM (+5), no screens 1 hour before bed (+5), warm shower before bed (+3), reading fiction (+3). These stack but cap at +15 total.

1.5 Napping

A character can take a nap during the day to partially offset sleep debt. A 20–30 minute nap clears 0.5 days of sleep debt. A 60–90 minute nap clears 1 day of sleep debt but may cause sleep inertia (−2 to all rolls for 30 minutes after waking). Napping more than 90 minutes risks entering deep sleep and waking up groggy (−3 to all rolls for 1 hour).

💡 GM Tip: Sleep Is Narrative Fuel

Don't just track sleep mechanically — use it narratively. A character who's been pulling all-nighters for a week should be visibly different: dark circles, slurred speech, snapping at coworkers. Show, don't just tell, the consequences. This is what makes Reality RPG feel real.

🚿 §2. Hygiene

Personal hygiene is a daily requirement that most people take for granted — until it becomes a problem. In Reality RPG, hygiene is tracked as a Hygiene Score from 0–10, starting each character at 10 (freshly cleaned). Each day without proper hygiene, the score drops.

2.1 Hygiene Routine Mechanics

A full hygiene routine takes 30–60 minutes and costs one Hygiene Action per day. The routine consists of several components:

2.3 Hygiene Access Issues

Not everyone has easy access to hygiene facilities. This is a material inequality mechanic — characters without stable housing or bathroom access face real challenges:

🏠 Own bathroom — Easy. No penalty. Full routine available.
🏢 Shared bathroom (dorm, shared house) — Moderate. Roll 2d10 (TN 35) each morning to get bathroom time. Failure = delayed routine, −1 to Hygiene Score that day.
🏢 Public restroom (gym, gas station) — Hard. Limited time, poor conditions, possible cost ($5–$15 for gym access). TN 50 to complete a full routine. Hygiene Score decays 1 extra point per day.
🚰 Water fountain / sink only — Very Hard. Can only wash face and hands. No shower possible. Hygiene Score decays 2 points per day. Must find a shower within 3 days or suffer health consequences.
🏕️ Homeless / no access — Extreme. Hygiene Score decays 3 points per day. Must find a shelter shower, 24-hour gym, or public bath within 2 days. Roll 1d20 each week; on 1–3, contract a serious infection requiring medical care.
📝 Example: Jamal's Hygiene Crisis

Jamal lost his apartment and is couch-surfing between friends. His host's bathroom is shared with 3 other people and often occupied. On Tuesday morning, Jamal rolls 2d10 = 62 against TN 35 to get bathroom time. Failure — he can only splash water on his face. His Hygiene Score drops from 7 to 5. By Thursday, it's at 3. His boss notices he smells and asks him to "freshen up." On Friday, his score hits 1. He can't keep showing up to work like this. He needs to find a gym membership, a shelter, or a friend with a spare bathroom — fast.

3.6 Hydration

Characters must drink at least 6–8 glasses of water per day. Neglecting hydration causes a cumulative −1 to all rolls per day of insufficient water intake (max −3). Excessive alcohol or caffeine consumption does not count toward hydration and actively worsens dehydration.

💡 Quick Reference: Daily Food Budget

A single person needs roughly $300–$600/month for adequate food on a budget. Below $200/month, nutrition quality drops significantly. Above $800/month allows for high-quality, varied eating. Characters earning minimum wage (~$1,500–$2,000/month after taxes) must allocate ~20–30% of income to food alone.

💼 §4. Work and Employment

For most characters, work is the central structure of their day — and their primary source of income. Employment in Reality RPG is a complex system involving job hunting, performance, relationships, career progression, and the emotional toll of labor.

4.1 Job Finding Mechanics

4.4 Overtime Effects

Overtime pays more but costs health and sanity. Each overtime hour beyond 8:

  • Earns 1.5× hourly rate (if legally required)
  • Generates +1 Stress Point per hour
  • Reduces available time for sleep, hygiene, meals, and leisure
  • After 60+ hour weeks for 3+ consecutive weeks: roll 1d20; on 1–4, gain 1 point of Burnout (permanent −1 to all rolls until character takes 1+ weeks of vacation)

4.5 Unemployment

Being unemployed is a chronic stressor. Each week of unemployment:

  • +3 Stress Points
  • Loss of income (must use savings or assistance programs)
  • Loss of employer-provided health insurance (in the US)
  • Social stigma: −2 to first-impression Social checks after 2+ months
  • Skill atrophy: −1 to job-relevant skills per month of unemployment

💡 Time Is Money — Literally

A 2-hour daily commute = 10 hours/week = 40 hours/month = one full extra job per month spent in a car or on a bus. Over a year, that's 520 hours — or roughly 3 weeks of life spent commuting. Characters with long commutes have less time for everything else: sleep, exercise, socializing, hobbies. This is a real quality-of-life mechanic.

The most devastating aspect of home maintenance is the cascade: miss rent → get evicted → lose stable housing → can't maintain hygiene → lose job → can't pay any bills → homelessness. This is not melodrama — it's the actual mathematical reality of financial precarity. Characters should feel the tension of managing limited resources against compounding obligations.

⚖️ Rule: Leisure Is Required

If a character goes 3 consecutive days without any leisure activity, they gain +3 SP automatically. If they go 7 consecutive days without leisure, they gain +5 SP and must make a 2d10 + RES check (TN 55) or suffer a Burnout Event (see Mental Health page). Characters cannot "grind" 24/7 — the human body and mind will not allow it.

7.2 Hobbies as Skill Development

Time spent on hobbies can improve skills. Each week a character dedicates 5+ hours to a specific hobby:

  • Roll 2d10 + relevant attribute against TN 60
  • Success = +1 to that skill (cap of +1 per month per skill)
  • Requires actual leisure time — cannot be done during work or sleep
📝 Example: Weekend Recovery

After a brutal week (15 SP accumulated), Mei uses her Saturday to recover: morning run at the park (🏃 2 SP cleared, +1 VIG), lunch with friends (👥 3 SP cleared), afternoon reading (📖 1 SP cleared), evening movie (🎬 1 SP cleared). Total: 7 SP cleared, +1 VIG restored. She still has 8 SP left, but she's functional. If she hadn't done any of this, she'd be at 15 SP — in the "Overwhelmed" zone with active penalties.

⏰ §8. Time Budgeting

This is the core mathematical constraint of Reality RPG. Every character has exactly 24 hours per day. Here's how the math works for a typical full-time working adult:

⚖️ The Daily Time Budget
😴 Sleep7.5h (31%)
💼 Work8.0h (33%)
🚗 Commuting1.5h (6%)
🍽️ Meals1.5h (6%)
🚿 Hygiene0.75h (3%)
🏠 Chores1.25h (5%)
⬆️ Obligations Total20.5h (85%)
🎮 Free Time (remainder)3.5h (15%)

Only 3.5 hours of free time per day. That's 24.5 hours per week. After accounting for weekends (when work is 0 but chores may increase), the average working adult has roughly 10–14 hours of genuine free time per week.

⚠️ The Time Deficit

When obligations exceed 24 hours, the character must sacrifice something. The GM asks: What do you cut? Sleep? (Sleep debt accumulates.) Meals? (Nutrition drops.) Chores? (Environment decays.) 👴 §9. Aging

Characters in Reality RPG age in real time. Unlike fantasy RPGs where a 25-year-old fighter and a 60-year-old fighter are mechanically identical, Reality RPG tracks the biological and social effects of aging. This is not just decline — aging also brings wisdom, experience, and stability.

💡 Aging Is Not Just Decline

Notice that INT and RES increase with age. Older characters are wiser, more experienced, and emotionally resilient. They may not run as fast or lift as much, but they make better decisions, handle stress better, and have deeper social networks. A 60-year-old character is not "worse" than a 25-year-old — they are different, with different strengths and vulnerabilities.

9.2 Health Events by Age

Each year, characters must make a Health Check. The TN increases with age:

70 Age 18–30 — TN 70. Roll 2d10 + VIG. On failure: Minor illness (cold, flu). 1 day of reduced function.
65 Age 31–40 — TN 65. On failure: Chronic issue begins (back pain, mild hypertension). −1 to relevant checks.
60 Age 41–50 — TN 60. On failure: Health condition develops (diabetes, heart issues, arthritis). Ongoing management required.
55 Age 51–60 — TN 55. On failure: Significant health event. May require surgery, long-term medication, or lifestyle change.
50 Age 61–70 — TN 50. On failure: Major health crisis possible. Hospitalization risk. May lose 1 attribute point permanently.
45 Age 71+ — TN 45. On failure: Severe health event. Each failure accumulates toward end-of-life events.

🏠

Career plateau or peak. May face age discrimination. Social circle stabilizes.

👴

Loss of work identity. −2 SP/day (no work stress) but +2 SP/day (loss of purpose). Net depends on character.

👵

Social isolation risk. Social checks at −2 due to generational gaps. +2 to wisdom-based checks.

⚖️ Final Rule: Life Is Cumulative

Every system on this page — sleep, hygiene, nutrition, work, commuting, home maintenance, leisure, and aging — interacts with every other system. A character who loses their job (§4) can't afford good food (§3), so their health declines, which means they can't exercise (§7), which accelerates aging (§9), which makes it harder to find a new job (§4). This is not game design — this is life. Reality RPG doesn't simulate a fantasy world. It simulates ours.
